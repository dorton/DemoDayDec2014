About the Academy

The Iron Yard powered is Houston's growing code school. The school represents a unique partnership between The Iron Yard and the Houston startup and tech communities, marrying credibility and experience with a strong understanding of the local technology landscape. Simply put, all of these groups are dedicated to the lifelong pursuit of knowledge, helping the city of Houston (and the state of Texas) thrive through grassroots initiatives, and giving people, especially women and other underrepresented groups, the chance to find professional success in the technology space.

What is Demo Day?

Students with Houston's first code academy have spent the last 12 weeks learning Ruby on Rails and Front-End Engineering. Today they will present their final projects to showcase the skills they've learned. Enjoy!

Cocktail Hour will come before and after Demo Day in START Houston. Appetizers and beer will be provided. We hope to see you there!

The Capstone Projects

SimplePW

One Password does it all! SimplePW is a password manager that helps you keep track of your usernames and passwords for all of your favorite sites. SimplePW is here to make your life Simple! With people using so many sites these days it is hard to keep track of all the different usernames and passwords. Just remember your SimplePW password and you will not have to remember another password again.

Features include a chrome browser extension, which allows you to access your sites, add new sites, and generate new passwords, and a web app that's you to add, edit, delete, and manage all of your site logins.

Technologies

Ruby on Rails, Javascript, jQuery, HTML/CSS, SASS/SCSS, Bourbon/Neat/Bitters/Refills and Bootstrap for styling, JSON, AJAX, Customized Login, Browser Screenshot API

Affluentsy

Affluentsy was inspired by a previous assignment drawing data from Etsy's API, as well as from curated lists of weird products from online stores. It pulls the most expensive items (which can often be the strangest) from the online store, and provides options for the user to curate that experience. On the site, you'll be able to view information about the product, share the item on social networks, and of course visit the actual product page on Etsy. The design of the site drew inspiration from other high-end retailers (given the nature of the products), and is built to work seamlessly on devices of all sizes.

Technologies

HTML, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Promise-based architecture with JS Prototypes, lodash/Underscore, FontAwesome, Photoshop, Etsy API, Node.js, Heroku, Myth, templating

SortaCloud

SortaCloud is a web application for anyone who has photos across multiple cloud services. Logging in to many different accounts just to find a particular photo can be a hassle. SortaCloud is a place you can view all your photos across all cloud services, rather than just one. Once logged in you are able to connect each cloud service. Currently, SortaCloud support Facebook, Dropbox and Flickr. The future will allow for additional services and the ability to create/share galleries.

Technologies

Music Match

People who enjoy music are always looking to explore their musical horizons. Music Match gives a user the ability to explore new music. It accepts either a music genre or an artist and generates a playlist based on user input. The user can use an individual song from an existing playlist to refine the selection in subsequent queries. Music Match is an interactive, back-end, ruby-based project that relies upon the Echo Nest for song selection and relies on Spotify to play previews of selected songs.

Technologies

Ruby, Echo Nest API, Spotify API, TDD, JSON

Chartography

Chartography is meant to make finding your next concert easy. It uses the Songkick API to provide when and where your favorite musicians are playing next. It displays all this information on a Map powered by Leaflet.js, and while your deciding about where you want to go for your next concert, you can listen to a music selection provided the Spotify Play-Button. It is an open source Ruby-on-Rails project at https://github.com/scarecrow182000/Chartography and coming soon as a live site at http://chartography.herokuapp.com/.

Technologies

Foundation5 by Zurb, Leaflet.js, Songkickr, RSpotify
